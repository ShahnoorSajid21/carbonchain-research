## Abstract

Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) face significant barriers when participating in Voluntary Carbon Markets (VCMs). One of the major challenges is the **"1 tonne barrier"**, where traditional carbon registries require carbon credits to be retired in minimum units of one tonne of CO₂e. For many SMEs, this amount exceeds their actual emissions and available sustainability budgets.

This project presents a **Blockchain Based Carbon Credit Fractionalization Framework** that enables verified carbon credits to be divided into smaller, tradable units. Using EVM compatible smart contracts, a verified 1 tonne carbon credit is securely locked and fractionalized into **1,000 micro tokens**, each representing **1 kilogram of CO₂e**.

The framework allows SMEs to offset emissions with greater precision, improves accessibility to carbon markets, and generates an immutable on chain compliance record.

# System Architecture

The framework consists of three primary smart contracts that work together to enable secure carbon credit fractionalization.

## 1. CarbonCreditNFT.sol

### Purpose

Represents a verified carbon credit worth **1 tonne of CO₂e** using the ERC 721 standard.

### Key Features

• Registry retirement verification through a two phase commit process.
• Stores a unique retirement hash generated by the off chain carbon registry.
• Prevents duplicate retirement claims.
• Eliminates replay attacks and double spending risks.

### Responsibilities

• Mint verified carbon credit NFTs.
• Store registry retirement evidence.
• Maintain ownership records.

## 2. CarbonVault.sol

### Purpose

Acts as the secure tokenization bridge between the ERC 721 carbon credit and ERC 20 fractional tokens.

### Key Features

• Locks deposited carbon credit NFTs.
• Mints exactly **1,000 Carbon Kilogram (CKG) tokens** for each deposited NFT.
• Uses OpenZeppelin's `ReentrancyGuard`.
• Records the original depositor address.
• Allows NFT withdrawal only after all fractional tokens are returned.

### Responsibilities

• Asset escrow.
• Fractionalization management.
• Deposit tracking.
• Secure redemption process.

## 3. CarbonMicroToken.sol

### Purpose

Represents fractional carbon ownership where:

```text
1 CKG = 1 Kilogram CO₂e
```

### Key Features

• ERC 20 compliant.
• Uses Role Based Access Control (RBAC).
• Only the vault contract can mint tokens.
• Governance functions are protected through a dedicated role.
• Supports carbon retirement and token burning.

### Retirement Event

When tokens are retired, the contract emits:

```solidity
CarbonRetired(
    retiree,
    kilogramsOffset,
    timestamp,
    esgReference
)
```

This creates an immutable compliance record on the blockchain.

# Economic Simulation

The repository includes a Monte Carlo simulation that evaluates the effectiveness of the proposed framework compared to traditional over the counter carbon markets.

## Methodology

The simulation generates:

• 10,000 synthetic SMEs
• Five emerging economies:
 • Pakistan
 • Bangladesh
 • Nigeria
 • Indonesia
 • Brazil

### Market Modeling

Carbon prices are modeled using **Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM)**:

```text
μ = 0.15
σ = 0.65
```

Calibration is based on 2023 voluntary carbon market data.

### Real World Constraints

The simulation incorporates:

• Crypto wallet penetration
• DeFi adoption readiness
• Regulatory acceptance
• Market accessibility barriers

## Results

The simulation demonstrates:

Elimination of the carbon credit rounding penalty

Greater accessibility for SMEs

More precise emissions offsetting

Improved affordability

**+2.08 percentage point increase in SME financial eligibility** compared to traditional carbon market participation methods

# Security Testing

The protocol is tested using Hardhat, Ethers.js, and Chai.

## Test Cases

### 1. Unauthorized Fractionalization

Verifies that only NFT owners can fractionalize assets.

### 2. Double Fractionalization Prevention

Ensures an already locked NFT cannot be fractionalized again.

### 3. Insufficient Token Balance

Prevents users from retiring more carbon tokens than they own.

### 4. Replay Attack Protection

Rejects reused registry retirement hashes.

### 5. Privilege Escalation Protection

Verifies that only governance accounts can perform administrative actions.

### 6. Input Validation

Rejects invalid or empty retirement hashes.

# Research Contribution

This framework introduces a practical blockchain based mechanism for democratizing access to voluntary carbon markets. By converting 1 tonne carbon credits into 1 kilogram micro assets, the protocol removes the traditional entry barrier faced by SMEs and enables transparent, auditable, and cost effective carbon offset participation.

The proposed architecture combines:

• Carbon credit tokenization
• Secure smart contract escrow
• Fractional ownership
• On chain retirement verification
• Economic feasibility analysis

to create a scalable solution for expanding SME participation in global sustainability initiatives.
